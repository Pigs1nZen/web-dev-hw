How to do an Ollie

By: Colin Smith

Invented by Alan Ollie Gelfand in the late 1970s, the Ollie is one of the first tricks a skateboarder will learn, and is essential in order to master the extreme sport. Since its inception, the Ollie has completely changed the direction of skateboarding into the sport we now know today.


Alan did an ollie like no other person. No one to this day has done ollies like him, and if you saw him back then it was so effortless and no one could copy that. He was definitely an original. An originator.- Craig Snyder, Author of A Secret History of the Ollie, Volume 1: the 1970s


What you need in order to succeed (Everything else is optional recommended):

Skateboard
Patience
Helmet
Knee pads
Elbow pads
Wrist guards
How-To Steps
Step 1 Step on the board.
Step 2 Adjust your feet with your front foot near the middle of the board and your back foot on the tail of the board.
Step 3 Bend your knees and crouch down.
Step 4 Jump into the air, first with your front foot, and after with your back foot.
Step 5 Jump up, and kick down the back of your board at the same time.
Step 6 Slide your front foot as soon as you start jumping.
Step 7 Pull your knees up towards your chest.
Step 8 Level out with both when your front foot has slid all the way up, keeping pressure on the board as it begins to fall down.
Step 9 Try to land both feet at the same time, preferably with your feet as close to their respective trucks as possible.
